Minor comments:

- Section 4 says: "When an NRS is included in an ICN architecture, security threats may
increase." I think what's meant is that the attack surface is increased when any new
component (in this case an NRS) is introduced.  Also, "These challenges must be overcome
by taking appropriate security measures into consideration." seems a bit of a useless
thing to say.

- I can think of two security/privacy issues that may be worth a mention but that are not
currently mentioned: 1) some equivalent to QNAME minimisation and 2) introduction of an NRS
might encourage development of a passive DNS equivalent with all the upsides and downsides
of that.

This and its companion document https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-irtf-icnrg-nrs-requirements/ passed ICNRG Last call in January, and has the support of the RG to progress toward publication as an Informational RFC. The two documents have had a long gestation period, and the RG debated multiple times the general importance of name resolution services as a distinct set of capabilities for ICN networks, and exactly what the scope of those services ought to be for different ICN architectures, including CCNx and NDN. While there is no strong consensus on that issue, from the last call comments (only a few) and the sidebar discussions, the chairs assess that on balance these documents provide useful background and a valuable discussion of various tradeoffs and approaches to the subject.

During pre-IRSG review by the IRTF Chair, some questions and issues were raised on the Arch Considerations draft. The authors prepared an updated version addressing these comments and also did an editing pass with the help of the document shepherd  to improve the writing.

We therefore believe this document is ready for IRSG Review and hopefully progression toward publication.
