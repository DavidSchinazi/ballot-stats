The IANA Functions Operator has completed its review of draft-melnikov-email-over-pmul-07. If any part of this review is inaccurate, please let us know.

We have two questions and a note about the registry actions for this document.

QUESTION: Should the new registry be placed at https://www.iana.org/assignments/mail-parameters?

QUESTION: The document states, "the Designated Expert(s) will be appointed and managed by the editors of this document together with the Independent Submissions Editor." In the future, should a designated expert step down, will the document editors always be included in the appointment of a new expert?

NOTE: The document states, "At IANA's discretion the new registry can instead be represented as an extra column in the existing "SMTP Service Extensions" registry." We don't believe this can be done, as it appears that a modification to that registry would require Standards Action or an IESG-Approved Experimental RFC (see https://www.iana.org/assignments/mail-parameters).

We understand that when this document is sent to us for processing, the only action required of us will be to create the following registry:

Registry name: Multicast Email SMTP Extensions
Registration Procedure: Specification Required

The registry will also include a column clled "Notes." For registrations that include one or more asterisks in their "Status" field, we'll remove the asterisk(s) and add the text of one of the notes listed below to the "Note" field.

(*) - SMTP CHUNKING MUST be supported on the receiving SMTP side of a
SMTP-to-MULE gateway and MAY be used on the sending side of MULE-to-
SMTP gateway.  A MULE relay doesn't need to do anything special for
this extension.

(**) - ENHANCEDSTATUSCODES extension is supported by including
relevant status codes in DSN [RFC3461] reports.

(***) - The AUTH parameter to MAIL FROM command is "supported", but
the rest of AUTH extension is not applicable to MULE.
