draft-cooley-cnsa-dtls-tls-profile has been presented for publication as
an Informational RFC on the Independent Submission Stream.

This document is one in a sequence produced to describe profiles of IETF
security RFCs for use in US Government systems. The profiles all copy or
tighten the BCP 14 language and serve to remove some options and choices
so that security is preserved or increased compare to the original RFCs.

The documents are published for two purposes:
- to facilitate participation in US Government systems by implementers
- to present the profiles as more widely available should other
  institutions wish to copy them.

The document makes the purpose of publication clear.

Other documents in this series include:
- draft-jenkins-cnsa-cert-crl-profile (RFC 8603)
- draft-jenkins-cnsa-cmc-profile (RFC 8756)
- draft-turner-sodp-profile (work in progress)
- draft-turner-ccmib (work in progress)
- draft-jenkins-cnsa-smime-profile (work in progress)

This document was reviewed Jonathan Hammell, Joe Salowey, Jim Schaad and
the ISE causing the four revisions of the draft. Details of the reviews
can be provided on request.

There are no IANA actions requested by this document.
