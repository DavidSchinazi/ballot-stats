## COMMENTS

Éric always loves when one author use UTF-8 and non-ASCII characters ;-)

### Section 1

Use past tense in `participant surveys indicate satisfaction` ?

### BCP14

As often, still wondering about the use of BCP14 terms in an informational document.

### Section 2

Please expand "WHO" ;-)

### Section 3.1

Like Rob and others, I wonder about having the same UTC time for European daylight saving (including EU, UK, and probably other countries).

PLEASE add Africa and South America in the mix (even with rough estimate or considering America as a whole).

Isn't there a conflict between:
- abstract: `These guidelines are based on the experience with online meetings during the COVID-19 pandemic.`
- section 3.1: `The following algorithm was not used in 2020 or 2021`

### Section 3.1.1

It took me a while to understand how table 2 was constructed :-O As a SW engineer, I would prefer to have some pseudo code like
```
  If last in-person == A then
      selected <- Night A
  else
      ...
```

### Section 3.4

s/number of possible conflicts/number of possible agenda conflicts/ ?

### Section 4

Suggest to drop 'recommendations' from the section title as I fail to see any recommendation in the section except 4.4 that deserves its own section (and not a subsection).

### Section 4.2

```
The experiment report [_112-EXPERIMENT] found that it led to a
  reduction in scheduling conflicts but also a slight drop attendance
  of the administrative plenary, partly due to insufficient awareness.
```
Should it be less assertive in the cause ? I.e., the lack of awareness is just an educated guess (that I share BTW).

### Section 4.3

Unsure how `preference for the amusements of home` reads for English speaker, but it sounds a little dismissive/arrogant to my French eyes....

I would not qualify "slight" the decrease in -00 submission though... But, I appreciate the use of the less assertive "might".

### Section 5

s/Toreless/Toerless/

Use plural form for 'review' and 'input' ?

[Ballot comment]
Hi,

I'm hoping that we won't need to make use of this draft for another pandemic or global issue any time soon.  There was previously some thought/discussion about having one meeting online per year anyway to reduce the IETFs carbon footprint - although I still think that online meetings seem to be distinctly less effective than when the significant majority of participants are meeting in person.

As for the actual document, just some minor comments:

I note that Europe Night times for Summer and Winter are the same, is this due to the EU's plans to abolish summer time?  If so, should there be a comment about summer time more generally.  E.g., if other countries followed suit and abolished summer time then would that change the timezones for other regions?

As a minor nit, section 3.1, referencing meetings happening after dinner, but at least in the UK, dinner is often referred to as the main meal of the day, so can happen at lunch time or in the evening, hence this is slightly ambiguous.

Does section 4.4 on experiments include experiments in the choice of online meeting timezone, as per section 3.1 and 3.1.1, or are those sections regarded as having community consensus and the IESG should follow until an update to this RFC is made?

Regards,
Rob

Subject: Last Call:  (Guidelines for the Organization of Fully Online Meetings) to Informational RFC


The IESG has received a request from the Stay Home Meet Occasionally Online
WG (shmoo) to consider the following document: - 'Guidelines for the
Organization of Fully Online Meetings'
  as Informational RFC

The IESG plans to make a decision in the next few weeks, and solicits final
comments on this action. Please send substantive comments to the
last-call@ietf.org mailing lists by 2022-12-13. Exceptionally, comments may
be sent to iesg@ietf.org instead. In either case, please retain the beginning
of the Subject line to allow automated sorting.

Abstract


  This document provides guidelines for the planning and organization
  of fully online meetings, regarding the number, length, and
  composition of sessions on the meeting agenda.  These guidelines are
  based on the experience with online meetings during the COVID-19
  pandemic.




The file can be obtained via
https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-ietf-shmoo-online-meeting/
