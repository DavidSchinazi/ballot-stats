[Ballot comment]
I have read the document and it is very approachable despite the occasionally mind-bending subject matter. It serves as both an excellent primer for the quantum neophyte and a thoughtful analysis of the architecture, principles and goals of quantum networks. Regarding terminology, I did wonder if the concept of a quantum 'state plane', in preference to a 'data plane', would be helpful to more accurately describe the architecture, and avoid confusion between classical and quantum links. I was also expecting to read something about requirements for and challenges of synchronisation between classical and quantum links but I didn't find that in the document.

I was asked to shepherd this document by the QIRG Chairs, who are both conflicted as authors of the document. I am an intermittent participant in the work of QIRG, although have not recently been following the progression of this work. I reread the draft (although did not do a through review) and examined the mailing list activity during and after Last Call on the document. There were a number of comments during Last Call from at least 4 people, so it does seem the document got a good review from enough people, with a uniformly positive assessment of the quality and maturity of the draft. therefore, it seems appropriate to recommend its progression to IRSG review.
