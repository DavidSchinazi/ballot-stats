[Ballot discuss]


I have a quick question to discuss, and will clear if you
tell me I've read the draft wrong, or that this isn't new or
that this is just too boring to even think about:-)

Is the following not a new security consideration even with
authenticated BGP? If an attacker can see the BoRR go by,
and can then force the TCP connection down before the EoRR
is seen (and stop new TCP connections coming up for as long
as the purge timer), the receiver marking the routes as
stale with a timeout before purging could cause the receiver
to purge all the sender's routes.  I guess receivers could
maybe detect this (because they're no longer hearing from
the sending peer and have only marked the routes as stale
because of the BoRR) and not purge the routes in that
specific case, but I'm not sure if it'd be worth noting
that. Or perhaps this is not a new threat?
